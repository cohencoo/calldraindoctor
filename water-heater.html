COMMON WATER HEATER PROBLEMS



You can repair some water heater problems without calling a service professional, but some are better left to experts. We can thoroughly inspect your system to decide whether a repair or a replacement will be the best for your situation.

Here are some of the most common water heater problems homeowners face.

  • No Hot Water. This is a common problem that, luckily, is easy for us to fix. If you are not getting hot water when you turn on the faucets, it could be because your pilot light is damaged. Replacing the pilot light and its additional elements is routine for a professional, so we advise you to let them handle the removal and installation of the new parts.
  • Little Hot Water. Is your water tank producing hot water but not enough to complete your tasks? This is a common problem, especially with older water tanks, but nothing we can’t handle. We can usually solve this problem by fixing the thermostat or changing faulty parts.
  • Bad Odor. Bad smell coming from the water means the water contains bacteria. Most homeowners describe the smell as a rotten egg-type scent, and it is very unpleasant. While you can solve the problem by filling the tank with clean water and hydrogen peroxide, it is better to call our water heater repairs to inspect the problem thoroughly.
  • Leaking Water Tank. If you notice a leak in your bathroom or basement, call the experts at Drain Doctor LLC to replace the tank before it causes more damage to your home. Even small tanks aren’t something you want to ignore.


Knowing when to call a professional water heater repair and replacement expert can help you avoid an even bigger catastrophe and expensive repairs in the future.

WATER HEATER REPLACEMENT



If your water heater is out-of-date (older than 20 years), it’s probably time to upgrade to a new model. Energy-efficient water heaters will provide enough hot water for the whole family without being extremely expensive.

After our fill inspection, we will provide you with a few different water heater options and discuss which type will be best to install for your property. We can install both conventional and tankless water heaters, depending on your needs and budget.

Conventional water heaters have a storage tank and electric or gas-powered heating elements, while tankless water heaters are more modern and are quite popular among homeowners today. They heat water only when it is needed and are highly energy efficient. Capacity is essential when choosing a tankless water heater, so make sure to consult with our technicians before purchasing one for your property.
